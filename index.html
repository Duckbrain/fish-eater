How to play Fish Eater:

In Fish Eater you play as a little fish in a big pond. You must become the best by becoming the biggest. Fish Eater is mainly played by using either the arrow keys; the W, A, S, and D keys; or the new touch screen joystick. You may use the keys to move in any direction in the water. Your goal is simple: eat smaller fish to become bigger. Fish are colored according to if you may eat them or not.

Controls

  • The directional arrow keys or the WASD keys can be used to control the fish
  • Touch anywhere on the game area and drag to make a joystick.
  • Press T to remove your powerup.
  • Touch anywhere on the game area twice in a row to cancel your powerup. If you have no powerup it wil pause. Tap again to unpause.
  • Click on the game area or press the spacebar to toggle the pause.

The colors are as follows.

  • Yellow: This is you. You control this fish.
  • Green: These fish can be eaten to make you bigger and give you health.
  • Purple: These fish can be eaten to make you bigger, give you health, and give you a temporary power-up. More on power-ups later.
  • Red: These fish can not be eaten to make you bigger and will take away health if you touch them.

To eat a fish, you simply run into it's body with your body. Tails have no effect when touched.

The power-ups are as follows.

  • Fly: This will let you soar through the air as if it was water.
  • Fast: They can't eat you if they can't catch you. This lets you can accelerate twice as fast and jump high off-screen.
  • Heal: You magically start to heal very quickly.
  • Control Song: This eerie song will force fish to swim to the surface, but be careful, some are too smart to fall for the trick.
  • Invincibility: This power-up will let you swim right past the big red fish.
  • Time Stop: This can help pick up some easy snacks or avoid danger.
  • Pac-man: No fish is safe from your hunger.
  • Super: This is the ultimate power-up, but can you make it last long enough?

About Fish Eater

Version 4.0.0

Fish Eater is a little game developed by me, Jonathan Duck.

Technical: Fish Eater was first written in C# as a quick little game drawn with GDI+. When I read about HTML5 and the canvas, I decided to try it out. The first release of the Chrome App was a literal copy and paste, then patch it up just enough to work in JavaScript. It has since been rewritten from scratch.
